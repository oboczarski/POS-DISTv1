The Positional Paradigm Transition

The NFL is always adjusting. Defensive coordinators build rosters to counter the offensive structures they see most often, then offensive coordinators adjust again to attack those defenses. That cycle never stops.

For more than a decade, the league moved heavily toward pass-first football. Offenses lived in 11 personnel, spread the field with three wide receivers, and forced defenses to get smaller, faster, and more comfortable playing nickel as a base package. Dynasty strategy followed that same direction. Wide receiver became the position most managers wanted to build around because of longevity, stability, and the pass-heavy scoring environment.

"That logic still has merit, but it is no longer complete. The league has started shifting back toward heavier offensive personnel, especially 12 and 13 personnel. That change has a direct effect on positional opportunity, fantasy scoring distribution, and dynasty roster construction."

Deep Dive Positional Analysis

How Heavier Structures Redistribute Tactical Opportunity

When offenses move away from 11 personnel and increase 12/13 personnel, the structure of the field changes. There are fewer wide receivers on the field, more tight ends attached to formations, more blocking surfaces, and a greater ability to run efficiently without becoming predictable.

Running Backs

Running backs are the biggest winners from heavier personnel. More 12 and 13 personnel generally means more run-friendly offensive structures. Extra tight ends create better blocking angles, allow offenses to attack lighter defensive boxes, and help teams stay multiple without tipping run or pass as clearly. That matters because the RB position is volume-sensitive and environment-sensitive.

Beneficial Impacts:
  • More rushing volume
  • Better rushing efficiency
  • More sustainable offensive drives
  • More red-zone rushing opportunities
  • More team commitment to the run game
  • More value concentrated in high-end workhorse backs
VOLUME & ENVIRONMENT SENSITIVE
Wide Receivers

Wide receivers are the position most negatively affected by the shift. In 11 personnel, three wide receivers are on the field. In 12 personnel, that drops to two. In 13 personnel, it drops to one. That alone reduces route availability, snap availability, and target opportunity for WR depth across the league.

This does not kill elite wide receivers. True alpha target earners still matter. But it does compress the position. The WR3/WR4 types on NFL teams become more fragile for fantasy because they are no longer guaranteed the same route volume when offenses are willing to play heavier.

INVERSE ENVIRONMENT CORRELATION
Tight Ends

Tight ends also benefit from the league’s heavier personnel trend, but the fantasy boost is more complicated than it is for running backs. More 12 and 13 personnel means more tight ends on the field. This increases overall TE snaps and creates more paths to relevance for athletic, versatile, or formation-flexible players.

However, adding tight ends to the field does not automatically mean fantasy production becomes more concentrated. In many cases, the extra snaps are spread across TE2 and TE3 types rather than flowing heavily to one primary fantasy option.

REAL-WORLD VS FANTASY SPLIT
Quarterbacks

Quarterbacks are affected by heavier personnel, but usually less dramatically. More 12 and 13 personnel can reduce raw pass volume, which slightly lowers the overall fantasy ceiling for quarterbacks. Fewer three-WR formations generally means fewer spread-out passing looks and fewer plays designed purely around volume.

At the same time, heavier personnel can also help quarterbacks in other ways. It can improve play-action efficiency, create easier reads, force defenses into heavier packages, and help offenses stay balanced. That can offset some of the lost volume.

SECONDARY OFFSETS APPLIED

Role-Based TE Differences

There is also a role-based difference. Heavier personnel packages are often built for versatility, not just receiving volume. Tight ends can run routes, stay attached as blockers, chip edge rushers, help in pass protection, or create run/pass ambiguity before the snap. That real-life value matters, but it does not always translate cleanly to fantasy points.

In lighter personnel, running backs are more often used as extra blockers in pass protection. In heavier 12 and 13 personnel, the additional tight end can fill more of that responsibility. That helps the offense function, but it can cap the fantasy impact for the position if those snaps are not turning into routes and targets.

The Positional Formula

"So the TE position does benefit, but not as cleanly as RB. The biggest real-world benefit goes to offenses. The biggest fantasy benefit goes to running backs. Tight ends rise directionally because they are on the field more often, but the fantasy scoring impact is less direct unless the player is also commanding meaningful target volume."

Meta Progression Tracking

Chronological Evolution Blueprint

Trace the chronological progression from initial unnoticed indicators to a complete, structural fantasy takeover.

01

2022 - 2023: Rumblings

Things began taking shape slowly in 2022 and early 2023, but there wasn’t enough data to draw any major dynasty conclusions. While there were small spikes in RB production and heavier personnel usage, the evidence remained well within historical norms. There was nothing to indicate these fluctuations were anything more than typical season-to-season volatility.

The shift became much more meaningful late in 2023 and into 2024. After the 2023 season, our positional analysis still leaned toward wide receiver as the better long-term dynasty build. The data supported WR, but not by as much as expected. That was the important part. The WR edge was no longer significant.

02

2024: Trend Set

By early-to-mid 2024, the trend was no longer something to ignore. 2024 was the first year the trend began gaining real ground, as the league's transition to heavier personnel started showing its first undeniable statistical anomalies. The old consensus spent a decade preaching that wide receivers were the only safe foundation for a dynasty build. In 2024, those foundations quietly began to crack as the ground game started weaponizing itself.

The Top 48 Shift: For the first time in over two decades, running backs gained massive ground, outnumbering wide receivers inside the top 48 fantasy scorers. This marked a significant shift in the landscape, resulting in the highest number of running back finishes in the top 48 since 2008.

The Squeeze at the Top: Within the Top 24, wide receivers plummeted, tying their lowest representation of the last 20 years. This aligned with a broader historical shift, as RBs claimed more total slots than WRs across the Top 24, Top 36, and Top 48 formats in the same season—a quiet coup unseen in over two decades.

Top 60 Erosion: Despite still holding a lead over RBs inside the Top 60, WRs hit a staggering two-decade low in overall presence. 2024 proved that a massive structural shift was actively forming under our feet. But while 2024 was the setup, it was merely the spark.

03

2025: The Breakout & Changing of the Tide

Ladies and gentlemen, running backs are not just back. They are operating at a level of dominance we have not seen in over 2+ decades! 2025 was without a doubt the changing of the tid. Let’s address the elephant in the room: in fantasy football, we occasionally see statistical outliers. We have all seen years where a tiny handful of legendary, superhuman running backs have massive seasons and temporarily inflate the Top 12 rankings. That is an anomaly.

What happened a bit in 2024 but completely in 2025 was not an anomaly. It was a complete, structural change in the Fantasy Football Landscape the likes of which we have not seen in over 20+ years.

For the first time in over two decades, running backs outscored wide receivers across every single major ranking tier in the exact same year: the Top 12, Top 24, Top 36, Top 48, and Top 60 . This is the ultimate milestone. When running backs outperform wide receivers across all ranges, it means the entire league-wide environment has shifted. This isn't just about the elite superstars winning at the top; it proves that the running backs down the fantasy roster—the RB2s, and flex plays—are ALSO out producing their wide receiver counterparts throughout the ranks.

04

2026: Outlook

If you were waiting for a sign to fully commit to the running back renaissance, the NFL didn’t just drop a hint—they shattered the glass. The draft wasn’t a mere confirmation of a trend continuing; it was a historic sledgehammer to old-school fantasy gospel.

In a lackluster pass-catching TE Class, but strong blocking-TE Class, The NFL just selected THE ALL-TIME RECORD number of tight ends in the Draft.. Let that sink in. This isn’t a subtle structural tweak. Of all TE draft classes, to break the record # of TE taken.. Is nothing short of a league-wide declaration of war on light defensive packages.

And again, make no mistake: this wasn’t a class of oversized wide receivers. These are physical monsters, elite blockers, and athletic freaks built specifically to dominate the line of scrimmage. Best believe, NFL front offices did not load up on this heavy-hitting archetype to spread the field. They drafted them to force defenses into mismatches, lock into 12 and 13 personnel, and bully opponents.

This is an absolute guarantee for RB & WR production to continue on their trajectories.. likely even surpassing historical numbers from 2025. The draft didn't just support the shift—it slammed the door shut on the idea that this RB revival is a temporary fad. The blueprint for 2026 is locked in. While the NFL will eventually adjust down the road, 2026 certainly is in line to be the peak so far.

Tactical Caution

Challenging Outdated Fantasy Traps

Avoid Static Rules

The fantasy market is still a bit behind, clinging to outdated “fantasy expert” rules & strategies like "always build around wide receivers, due to longevity" While in the same breath telling you to “only think about players in a 3 year windows”..

Dont fall for those misleading traps:

The biggest dynasty edge is always adaptability. Static rules are dangerous. “Always build around WR.” “Never pay up for RB.” “RBs are replaceable.” Those ideas are useful in specific cases, but the environment has changed, & those top young RBs are irreplaceable.

In the current NFL meta, top young running backs should be prioritized more aggressively because they are benefiting from both individual talent and league-wide structural support. Wide receivers still matter, especially elite target earners, but the broader WR advantage has narrowed.

Eventual Regressions

When This Could Reverse

Cyclical Reality

This will not last forever.

The NFL is cyclical. Defenses will adjust. Roster construction will change again. Offensive coordinators will eventually find new ways to exploit the counters defenses create. At some point, the meta will shift away from this RB-friendly environment.

That reversal could begin as early as 2028, but it’s impossible to tell at this time especially with it still being relatively new.

Right now, the data points in one direction: heavier personnel is rising, RBs are benefiting, WR depth is being squeezed, and the dynasty market still has room to adjust.

Moving Forward

The Ground Leverage Thesis

Running backs are not just back. They are in a position we have not seen in at least the past two decades.

This is not nostalgia. It is not one random scoring spike. It is not just a few elite players carrying the position.

The NFL environment has changed. Offenses are using more size, more tight ends, and more run-friendly structures to counter defenses that were built to stop spread passing attacks. That has pushed more opportunity toward running backs while pulling opportunity away from wide receiver depth.

"That is why this matters for dynasty. The RB revival is not only about production after the fact. It is about understanding the structural reason behind the production before the market fully catches up. For dynasty, the takeaway is clear: while this trend continues, top young RBs should be valued as premium build-around assets."
